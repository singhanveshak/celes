UCC: A curse or a boon?

Dec, 2023

India has equal justice system for every individual with IPC, CPC and other substantive and procedural laws except a loophole of personal laws which vary from religion to religion. In this nation of cultural diversity and symphony, people have different rules and laws to follow according to their religions and community. With 110 crore Hindus,20 crore muslims,4 crore Christians and 3 crore Sikhs, would it ever be possible to bring UCC to existence? Imagine you live in a land where the same law applies to everyone despite their religions and community. Imagine you live in turkey, Tunisia, Albania or France. Let’s unravel the story of a legal revolution: The Uniform Civil Code(UCC), either it gets implemented or not.

WHAT IS UCC?

Uniform Civil Code does not mean that after implementation, everyone has to take seven rounds for marriage pr imposing one religion’s customs on another. Uniformity would mean registering all these marriages. Thus, The uniform civil code is a proposal in India to draft and execute personal laws of citizens which apply on all citizens of the nation despite their religion, sexual orientation and gender. The primary objective of UCC is to promote secularism and elimination of religious legal distinctions. Uniform civil code represents the concept of equal rights and justice without any bias. UCC would be legislative enforcement or interference to make fundamental rights more supreme.

HISTORY

India is a country of different culture and diversities with more than 1721 languages spoken here and different communities living throughout the nation. Before the independence of India, British raj reigned the country as well as the rules. British raj did introduce the set of personal laws mainly for Hindus and Muslims, the main religions of the nation. Even though they were the supreme power, they feared religious wars. They chose not to risk and interfere with the religious sentiments of the people of the communities. At the time, Goa was separated from India due to colonial rule and thus retained a common family law known as Goa civil code and is the only state in India with a uniform civil code in India. Ironically, the Goan civil code is not uniform as it has special provisions for different communities. For example; Bigamy is allowed to Hindu men if the wife does not deliver a child before the age of 25 or a male child till the age of 30. Post independence; In 1950s, election was fought and it was promised that the Hindu code bill will be passed. The process of creating the Hindu Personal Code involved extensive discussions and consultations with various stakeholders, including legal experts, religious leaders, social reformers, and representatives from the Hindu community. The objective was to address the inequalities and discriminatory practices prevalent in traditional Hindu personal laws and bring about social reforms. Hindu code bills were introduced which had special provisions and reformed personal laws in various communities and religions like Hindus. Jains, Sikhs, Buddhists but the bill exempted Christians, jews, Muslims and Parsis, being considered different and foreign communities than India. UCC rose as an important topic of interests in Indian politics following the Shah Bano case of 1985. People could not pick a side on making certain laws applicable to all citizens without breaking the fundamental rights of right to freedom of practice of religion. Afterwards, the debate rose then focused on the Muslim personal law which is partially based on the Sharia law, permitting unilateral divorce, polygamy.

PRESENT TIMES

Implementation on the uniform civil code across this nation is the work of India’s ruling party, Bhartiya Janta Party (BJP) BJP has mentioned UCC various times in their election manifesto and even won those elections but UCC remains pending. Personal laws cover marriages, divorces, inheritance and adoption. UCC was introduced in India twice, once in 2019 and once in 2020 but the strict opposition forced the government to take it back. In year 2023, it was introduced again by Mr. Narendra Modi as an answer to equality before the constitution and it is still facing a strong opposition from various communities. In march, when a PIL was filed in Supreme court to pass UCC, the court declared, “The court is a wrong forum to enact UCC and only the parliament can make a law on this.” “Think about it many times so that no storm shall come anywhere if steps are taken on it”, cited Kapil Sibal, a known Indian politician(Samajwadi party) and lawyer.

DILEMMA:

While Article 25 to 28 of the Indian constitution promise religious freedom to Indian citizens and allows religious groups to maintain their own affairs, article 44 of the constitution expects the Indian constitution to apply directive principles and common law for all Indian citizens while formulating national policies. Is UCC a Hindu-Muslim debate? The common people of India are not willing to fully understand the concept and think about it as a debate between the two religions on the basis of the historical differences and commotion. In reality, UCC is intended to remove the discriminatory and prejudicial laws which are clearly wrong. This law will be more beneficial for the female population as it would give equal marital rights to all.

VIEWS AND STANCE BY DIFFERENT PARTIES:

While BJP still stands with the implementation of UCC, there are various political parties with their views and stand.

• Congress: It will be difficult to implement UCC in a country like India where various communities and groups are governed by personal laws "On June 15 we issued a statement, but there is no change in stand on UCC. When there's a draft and a discussion we will participate and examine what is proposed. As of now, all we have is the Law Commission public notice for responses. The Congress reiterates the statement as nothing new has happened.”, cites Jairam Ramesh, Congress general secretary. • CPM: Any move to push the agenda of UCC, as is being done by the government directly and through its institutions, is counterproductive for the rights of women. Uniformity is not the guarantee for equality. • Shiv Sena: The Muslim Law Board should support the UCC as it will help the community, especially the women to come out of misery. • BSP: BJP has been trying to impose the agenda of the RSS on the people ever since it came to power at the Centre. •  RJD: If BJP wants to apply the Uniform Civil Code, they should first apply that in Jammu and Kashmir as they are in power there. •  "Firstly Prime Minister should inform the country what is the proposal for UCC and on what issues does he want uniformity. Until a proposal is put forward, there is no need for a debate”, said Kapil Sibal (July 1, 2023)

It is important to note that the views of the political parties may or may not be biased and their official statements are merely their views and concerns about the implementation of a revolutionary cultural law.

DRAWBACKS OF UCC:

As important as it is to make a law, it is more important to know how to implement it. UCC has several problems from describing it and it's goal properly to making it as a law and even putting it into function. Before UCC is implemented there should be a mindset and a culture that every Indian citizen is a Indian citizen and laws are not from the perspective of anyone's birth. This is very difficult and challenging as it will not be accepted widely. While UCC is a major public diversity concern;

  1. There is a wrong mass perception about the law that it is against the religious freedom of people.
  2. It is a sensitive topic.
  3. Practical difficulties due to diversity
  4. the help of state, it would be difficult to implement it.
  5. The transition from multiple personal laws to a single uniform code could have unintended consequences on various aspects of society. It might disrupt existing social norms, familial relationships, and inheritance patterns, which could lead to unforeseen challenges and grievances
The states are divided on the basis of their stance on UCC. States like Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Haryana, Gujarat, Assam and Maharashtra stand with it while states like Meghalaya, Nagaland and Mizoram oppose it. The 21st law commission had declared the issue not desirable or necessary due to the practical problems like; In Hindu marriages, the marriage is considered a holy sacrament; in Christians, divorce is stigmatised; in Muslims, marriage is a contract and in Parsis, registration of marriages is important. Thus it becomes important to respect all these attitudes and one religion cannot be imposed on another. Now even if the UCC is not brought to existence, the state can still remove injustices by making amendments in personal laws. Filtering the regressive aspects in personal laws is also a good alternative.

All in all, It’s of significance to note out that there is also a difference between uniformity and diversity. Also, The personal laws must change according to the constitution being the ultimate supreme authority to remove derogatory and discriminatory laws. While the supporters say it is important to create a cohesive legal framework, the objectors say that it is a threat to religious practices and poses insensitivity towards religions. Given the sensitivity of the issue, it is important to look for a middle ground that respects individual rights while nurturing a sense of unity.
